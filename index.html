OVERVIEW

Mobile Task Force Beta-1 is a highly classified internal security unit specializing in counter-intelligence, anti-infiltration, and data-leak mitigation. The primary mandate of Beta-1 is to identify, track, and neutralize rogue elements or foreign intelligence assets operating within Foundation personnel, preventing critical data streams from reaching external Groups of Interest (GoIs).

Due to the extreme operational sensitivity of their missions, the identities of all active operatives, exact tactical strength, and specific headquarters locations are entirely scrubbed from main database pools.

OPERATIONAL INTEGRATION

While technically designated as a Mobile Task Force under the Department of Task Forces (DTF), Beta-1 functions almost entirely as an active-clandestine wing of the Internal Security Department (ISD). Because of its ghost-like operational profile, Beta-1 is widely considered a myth or urban legend among standard Foundation employees.

TACTICAL STRUCTURE

  • Undercover Divisions: Deep-cover agents embedded long-term into highly sensitive Foundation projects and sectors to monitor for systemic rot or personnel irregularities.
  • Internal Combat Divisions: Heavy tactical suppression units trained specifically for asymmetric close-quarters combat inside Foundation facilities. These squads are deployed exclusively to resolve internal armed mutiny, compromised site high-command scenarios, or specific ████████ containment breaches.

SECURITY PROTOCOLS

All field operations dictate the strict enforcement of multi-layered encryption keys, heavily compartmentalized operational knowledge, and aggressive recursive record purges. Following the successful closure of an operation, specialized data scrubbers erase all active digital and physical footprints to maintain absolute operational deniability across the ████ grid.
